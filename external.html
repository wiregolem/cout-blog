Books & Links

External Learning Resources

C++: The Complete Reference 4th edition
by Herbert Schildt - https://www.herbschildt.com
ISBN13: 978-0072226805
ISBN: 0-07-2226803

    An instant favorite, it doesn't baby it's readers, its not a guide to learn C++ in a day, nor is it a series of tutorials to make you feel as though you've learned something at the end. Instead, Herbert Schildt has written an extensive reference to the C++ language and it's subset C. Read it through and step toward mastery of the language or thumb it's clearly defined sections to look up a component of the language at your convenience. This book is invaluable. I've purchased it twice and would buy it again if necessary.
    However, the 5th edition of the book was never published, leaving the 4th edition quite behind on the newest features of C++. Still the book remains a priceless learning tool as the features in the book remain relevant.
    Herb's website is quite barebones and only shows his most recent works.

Internetworking with TCP/IP
Principles, Protocol, and Architecture
by Dr. Douglas E. Comer - https://www.comerbooks.com
Distinguished Professor of Computer Science at Purdue University and former VP of Research at Cisco

ISBN-13: 978-0136085300
ISBN-10: 0-13-608530X

    An in depth guide to everything that makes the internet possible. Not for the feint of heart. Reading this book requires dedication and passion. It has numerous volumes.
Volume II: Design Implementation, and Internals
Volume III: Client-Server Programming and Applications
The third volume comes in 5 flavors:
  • Linux/POSIX Socket Version
  • BSD Socket Version
  • AT&T TLI Version
  • Window Sockets Version
  • Window Sockets Version(International Edition)

Practical Guide to Linux Commands, Editors, and Shell Programming 4th ed.
by Mark Sobell - https://www.sobell.com
ISBN-13: 978-0134774602

    A really easy book to guide you through using the command line in linux. It's great reference for anyone starting out, or anyone who forgets a thing or two from time to time.
    It even covers BASH, Tcsh, Python, Perl, MariaDB, AWK, Sed, Emacs, and Vim!

W3Schools
Resegnes Data, Norway - https://www.w3Schools.com
    Run by Regsnes Data in Norway, W3Schools is without a doubt the best online reference for HTML and CSS. While W3Schools is named after the World Wide Web, it is in no way officially associated with W3C the World Wide Web Consortium, but it is incredibly comprehensive. Their website not only has references, but also tutorials with with interactive examples using their "TryIt Editor".
W3Schools also covers JavaScript, jQuery, Angular, AppML, Python, Java, SQL, PHP, ASP, XML, really it's a lot.

Khan Academy
Sal Khan Fournder of Khan Academy, Inc - https://www.khanacademy.org
    A non-profit organization created by Sal Khan, with educational videos originally made to tutor his cousin online. Sal's videos are short, and very concise. He masterfully delivers his broad knowledge a few minutes at a time in a way that is instantly digestible, regardless of age as long as you've covered any requisite topics. He covers what is arguably all of mathematics starting from the absolute basic foundations of arithmetic. If you've ever thought that Khan Academy was for kids, while the videos are each incredibly simple and colorful, you'll be pleasantly surprised to see how advanced the topics become. They include statistics, differential calculus, and group theory. Khan academy has courses which cover numerous topics, each with a comprehensive syllabus that is easy to follow and quickly progress. There are videos regarding the sciences, finance, and even SQL. Khan Academy is the number one resource for self advancement and review of academia and the leading catalyst for the advancement of STEM education all over the world.
